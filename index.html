Fundamental ingredients of pastry

There are three fundamental ingredients in pastry and bakery, we refer to eggs, dairy and fats; These elements are different from each other, as are the recipes for which they are used and the results that are obtained, for this reason we have to delve a little deeper into all their characteristics.

Eggs

Eggs are used in bakery and pastry as part of recipes or as fundamental ingredients in certain preparations such as soufflés and meringues, this is due to the proteins contained in the egg white, in the case of meringues it is necessary to integrate air while we beat the egg whites with sugar.

Additionally, the egg also serves to make a natural glue that works for us when cooking, since it has a great ability to unite the ingredients.

Finally, the yolk also helps us to shine the surfaces of breads and cakes.

How to determine the freshness of eggs?

Easy! Use a container full of water, if the egg sinks like a stone, it is fresh and in good condition, but if it sinks slowly or floats, it is that it has lost moisture and may not be fresh, in which case it is better to discard it.
Dairy Products

Dairy is made up of a huge group of ingredients, including cheeses, creams, butter, and other products.

Breast milk is the first food we consume in life, as we grow older, we replace it with cow's milk or vegetable milk, which shows the enormous importance of dairy products in nutrition, for this reason so much time and effort has been devoted to the different presentations of dairy products such as cheeses, yogurts and creams of multiple flavors and consistencies.

The components of milk that interest us are: fats, casein and lactose.

Raw milk contains fat, this can be verified by leaving the milk in a tub for a few hours, because due to the repulsion that exists between water and oil, the milk fats rise and concentrate on the surface to finally become in a viscous liquid known as cream.

  • Caseins: Milk contains highly heat-resistant proteins known as caseins, these are capable of withstanding temperatures of up to 150 ° C, a characteristic that allows us to manufacture evaporated milk or powdered milk through various pasteurization systems. These ingredients are used a lot in baking.
  • Lactose: The natural sugar found in milk is called lactose and is currently used to prepare breads, cakes and desserts. It is a component that we must take into account in baking, since there are people who can be lactose intolerant, which will cause them to not be able to digest our desserts.

There are different types of milk that are used in baking, these are:

  • Whole milk: Term used to refer to raw milk, that is, milk that is freshly baked or in its natural state.
  • Skim milk: By separating the cream from the milk, a dairy with 1-2% fat content is obtained, which with more modern separation methods, can be reduced to 0.1-0.5% fat.
  • Evaporated milk: It is whole milk that has been reduced by half by evaporation, hence its name.
  • Condensed milk: Milk with a high content of added sugar, for this reason it does not need to be sterilized.
  • Powdered milk: Milk powder that is diluted with water to form a liquid, it is possible to create it thanks to the resistance that milk proteins have to high temperatures.
Fats and oils

These elements provide texture, moisture and flavor to the preparations, therefore they will be an essential element in your recipes, to understand it better, we can say that lipids are divided into two groups: oils and fats.

Oils differ because they are extracted from vegetable sources and are usually liquid, while fats can be products of animal or vegetable origin that, if they are at room temperature, are capable of acquiring a semi-solid state similar to a gel.

Importance of dough in confectionery

Now we will talk about another equally important aspect, we refer to the doughs and flours, these represent the bricks with which we will build a great diversity of desserts and sweet dishes, among which are cookies, cakes, breads, pies and many more products.

Flours can be classified by:

  • The type of cereal
  • Grinding and sifting
  • Amount of protein

On the other hand, yeasts and leavening agents are used for fermentation, these products can be both natural and chemical and help us to obtain different high-quality doughs, they also allow us to define certain characteristics such as their aroma, texture, volume and crumb.

The fermentation of the flours occurs when the yeast begins to consume the sugars (carbohydrates) that it naturally contains, this produces the formation of carbon dioxide (CO2 gas) and alcohol, which allows the dough to swell and acquire volume.

Without this procedure it would be impossible to form the crumb and crunchy crust of the breads.

Types of cakes

Cheesecake: It is a cheese cake prepared based on (as its name implies) cheese, cheddar, ricotta or cream cheese, it includes sugar and in some cases eggs, cream or fruits.

Cheesecake estilo New york. This delicious dessert has its origin in the ancient times of the Greeks and Romans, although the recipe is very similar to the American one, it is prepared with other types of ingredients.

In the United States it originated when a cheese company tried to make the cheesecake incorporating French Neufchatel cheese, this error gave rise to the renowned Philadelphia cheese and gave rise to the famous American-flavored cheesecake.

It is interesting to note that cities like Chicago have a version with sour cream and in St Louis it is filled with an extra layer.

Today we will teach you how to prepare this delicious recipe!

Ingredients

  • 400 g of vanilla cookies or plain cookies;
  • 140 g of unsalted butter, melted;
  • 350 g of granulated sugar;
  • 1.5 kg of cream cheese at room temperature;
  • 58 g of cornstarch;
  • 1 lemon zest;
  • 10 ml of vanilla extract;
  • 2 pieces of egg yolk;
  • 5 pieces of whole egg, and
  • 140 g of sour cream.

Step-by-step elaboration:

Preheat the oven to 175 ° C and grind the cookies in a processor to a powder, then add the melted butter.Use the bowl of the mixer and the paddle attachment to place the cream cheese and sugar, mix until perfectly incorporated. Then add the starch, zest of 1 lemon and vanilla, every so often stop the mixer to remove the mixture from the edges and bottom with the help of a spatula. Add the eggs and yolks one by one, mix very well and then add the sour cream. Cover the bottom and the wall of the mold with the dough resulting from the cookies and butter, then add the mixture and smooth the upper part with a spatula. Bake for about 50 to 60 minutes or until the cream only moves slightly in the center. Let cool perfectly, unmold and refrigerate 4 to 5 hours before serving.

Decoration with fruits in your cakes

Fruits are incredible ingredients that can be used to prepare a wide variety of sweet and sour dishes. In addition to providing flavor and color to our creations, they help us make them look more delicious and fresh.

Fruits can be classified into:

  • Climatic: They have the ability to ripen even if they are separated from the plant of origin, this characteristic is possible because they have a plant hormone called ethylene, whose function is to regulate the changes in color, flavor and texture of the fruits.
  • Non climacteric: Fruits that cannot continue to ripen after being separated from the plant of origin, as they do not contain ethylene.
The importance of sauces in baking

Sauces and coulis are responsible for giving the last touch of flavor, on the one hand, coulis are preparations based on cooked or raw fruits that can accompany desserts of chocolate, vanilla, coffee or intense flavors, while the sauces are prepared based on cream, butter, caramel or emulsified sauces.

In this field, there are no rules for the combination of flavors, this aspect totally depends on the confectioner, who can innovate as much as he wishes; However, we must always try to create an appropriate combination of flavors, in this way the contrast will be pleasant to the palate and the eye.

Both sauces and coulis are used to accompany different desserts such as cakes, tarts, ice creams, mousses, creams, petit four and many more variations.

Ideas for a good pastry recipe

Being clear about the most popular types of cakes is not the only thing you need to create the best recipes, that is why we have created some ideas that will work very well for you to achieve an exceptional one.

Get familiar with the measurements

Unlike recipes for traditional dishes, measuring conditions such as 1 cup or 2 tablespoons do not work in confectionery; measurements in grams are usually used more to prevent inconveniences when preparing your recipe.

Take care of the temperature of your oven.

Did you burn the cookies? Come on, we know that yes, know as well as possible the type of oven you use to correctly determine the temperature or the type of baking to prepare your favorite type of cake.

Adapt your recipes to the moment.

You can make desserts that are adapted for winter, such as; Condensed milk, hazelnut and raisin cakes or for the summer like; cold lemon pudding.

Respect aesthetics.

You can include some creative tricks in the recipe and decoration, the desserts that people usually choose are mostly determined by their visual impact.

Preserve tradition and experiment.

Never lose your taste for those well-known special recipes that are passed down through different generations.

Prepare desserts with the help of the little ones at home.

For the little ones, pastries and different types of cakes are the gateway to the incredible world of cooking.

Go beyond your oven.

Do not miss preparing delicious cakes because you do not have an oven or because it was damaged, this is your time to try making cold cakes, namely, for this type of cakes you do not need to use the oven. Some can even be prepared on the stove or in an electric oven.
